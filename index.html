About Me

I am a Ph.D. researcher at UC Berkeley specializing in LiDAR, remote sensing, and multimodal perception. My work focuses on developing scalable pipelines for urban-scale spatial data, integrating causal machine learning, and advancing multimodal perception for autonomous vehicles and resilient cities.

Research & Projects

Autonomous Systems & Perception

Multimodal Urban Perception for Autonomous Systems (UC Berkeley, Jan 2025– )

  • Developed a causal graph fusion framework integrating LiDAR, panoramic imagery, and satellite data.
  • Designed joint evaluation protocols for dynamic robustness and static scene understanding (SVF, occlusion).
  • Produced interpretable modality attributions to enhance safety-critical perception reliability.

Large-Scale LiDAR Processing for AV Line-of-Sight Analysis (UC Berkeley, Oct 2024– )

  • Built scalable Python pipelines to process 900+ LiDAR (LAZ) files in San Francisco, extended to 10+ U.S. cities.
  • Computed Sky View Factor and quantified occlusions from urban morphology for AV sensor placement.
  • Produced high-resolution DSM/DEMs for line-of-sight and coverage validation.

Autonomous Vehicle Collision Data Analysis (2025– )

  • Analyzed California DMV AV crash reports and geospatial infrastructure data.
  • Engineered features from road geometry, visibility, and infrastructure to identify high-risk zones.
  • Developed insights for safer AV deployment and urban design improvements.

Urban Environment & Resilience

Multimodal AI-Agent Framework for Urban Simulation (UC Berkeley, Feb 2025– )

  • Designed an AI-agent framework to simulate human-environment interactions at city scale.
  • Fused LiDAR morphology, street-view imagery, and satellite data for agent perception.
  • Integrated LLM-driven behavioral profiles to create diverse, realistic simulations.

Green Schoolyard Equity & Shade Access (UC Berkeley, 2024– )

  • Mapped and analyzed shade equity across New Mexico schoolyards using GIS and remote sensing.
  • Integrated tree canopy, built environment, and demographic data to assess exposure disparities.
  • Collaborated with Green Schoolyards America to inform equitable design interventions.

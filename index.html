About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in the Landscape Architecture and Environmental Planning program at the University of California, Berkeley. My research sits at the intersection of urban climate, geospatial data science, remote sensing, computer vision, and environmental justice. I am a member of the Geo-3M Lab and am supervised by Prof. Lu Liang.

I develop computational methods to understand how the built environment, vegetation structure, and urban form shape human experiences of heat, pollution, shade, visibility, and everyday environmental exposure. My work combines LiDAR, street-view imagery, satellite observations, GIS, and causal / machine learning methods to produce high-resolution, human-centered measures of urban environments.

More broadly, I am interested in how geospatial AI can support healthier, more equitable, and more climate-resilient cities.

Research Interests

  • Urban Climate & Environmental Exposure: heat exposure, thermal inequity, air pollution, cumulative human-environment exposure
  • 3D Urban Form & Vegetation: LiDAR-based urban morphology, tree canopy structure, volumetric greenness, sky view factor
  • Geospatial AI & Multimodal Sensing: integration of satellite imagery, street-view images, LiDAR, and mobility data
  • Causal & Explainable Machine Learning: interpretable modeling of urban environmental processes and socio-spatial disparities
  • Environmental Justice & Planning: equitable access to shade, cooling, green infrastructure, and healthier urban environments

Selected Projects

Pedestrian-Scale Sky View Factor Mapping with Street View and LiDAR

UC Berkeley · 2025–Present

  • Developing a human-centered framework to estimate Sky View Factor (SVF) from street-view imagery and 3D urban reconstruction.
  • Combining monocular geometry, panoramic imagery, and LiDAR benchmarks to validate pedestrian-level visibility of the sky.
  • Supporting urban climate, radiation, and thermal comfort studies with scalable street-level environmental indicators.

Large-Scale LiDAR Processing for Urban Environmental Analysis

UC Berkeley · 2024–Present

  • Building scalable Python-based workflows to process large LiDAR collections for U.S. cities.
  • Generating high-resolution DSM/DTM products and extracting metrics related to urban morphology, vegetation structure, and visibility.
  • Using 3D data to study street enclosure, shade potential, tree structure, and neighborhood-scale environmental conditions.

3D Vegetation Structure, Shade, and Environmental Equity

UC Berkeley · 2025–Present

  • Investigating how three-dimensional vegetation structure changes the way urban greenness is measured and experienced.
  • Developing metrics beyond conventional 2D canopy cover to capture vertical complexity, volumetric greenness, and pedestrian exposure.
  • Connecting urban vegetation structure to questions of distributional justice, cooling benefits, and greening inequities.

Mobility-Based Heat Exposure and Human-Centered Urban Simulation

UC Berkeley · 2025–Present

  • Designing computational frameworks to estimate how people experience heat and environmental risk during daily mobility.
  • Integrating mobility patterns, urban form, thermal environments, and behavioral heterogeneity into human-centered exposure models.
  • Exploring how AI-driven simulation can support more equitable and climate-responsive urban planning.

Green Schoolyards, Shade Access, and Child Environmental Equity

UC Berkeley · 2024–Present

  • Mapping and evaluating shade access and greening disparities across school environments using GIS and remote sensing.
  • Combining tree canopy, built environment, and demographic data to identify inequities in schoolyard heat exposure.
  • Collaborating with partners to support evidence-based, equitable design interventions for healthier school spaces.

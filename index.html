About Me

I am a Ph.D. researcher at UC Berkeley specializing in LiDAR, remote sensing, and multimodal perception. My work focuses on large-scale spatial data processing, causal machine learning, and advancing sensing systems for autonomous vehicles and urban resilience.

Research & Projects

  • Multimodal Urban Perception for Autonomous Systems — Developed causal graph fusion framework integrating LiDAR, camera imagery, and satellite data for robust AV perception.
  • Large-Scale LiDAR Processing — Built scalable pipelines to compute Sky View Factor and occlusion analysis from 900+ LiDAR files in San Francisco.
  • AI-Agent Framework for Urban Simulation — Created multimodal agent-based simulations for human–environment interactions, accepted for ACSP 2025.
  • Causal ML for Urban Analysis — Applied NLP (BERT) and causal ML to analyze >1M geotagged social media posts.
